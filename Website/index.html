Budget Your Expenses

Budgeting can be daunting at first, but with some practice you will find it becoming easier and easier with time.
  • Set some goals for you to work towards. You are more likely to hit your financial milestones by having specific targets to hit beforehand.
  • Write down your expenses and list them based off priority. Decide what things are absolutely needed versus things that are 'wants'. This will help you see what you can reduce in order to save more.
  • Write down important dates to make sure you are on top of bills and expenses that are coming up soon.

Managing Debt

Debt can get out of hand and leave us stressed and overwhelmed. There are a few considerations you can take so that you are on track to getting these debts in order and becoming debt free.
  • Find out how much debt you owe in total. You can't pay off your debts if you aren't tracking what needs to be paid.
  • Figure out how much you can pay. Using the budgeting tips we provided you can figure out how much you earn, how much is used for your general expenses, and finally how much is remaining. This amount is what you can use to start settling your debts.
  • Set an amount to pay for each debt and focus on paying more on the smaller debts. After paying them off continue to set aside the amounts you did for the smaller debts despite paying them off, but assign them to the next debt that is closest to being paid off, paying a larger sum overall but still setting aside the same amount. Overtime you will snowball and be paying off your debts a lot faster.
Financial Hardship
Managing Debt Financial Hardship

Financial Hardship

Everyone faces difficulty, it is part of being human and is something everyone will go through at some point in their lives. You don't have to go through it alone. In these tough times it is important to reach out to those that can help guide you through this rough patch. There are a few people you can reach out to.
  • Reach out to your lender. Tell them of your circumstances and try and get an arrangement that best suits your needs.
    • You can get a temporary financial hardship arrangement where you and your lender agree to reduce your payments or defer them for a period of time.
    • You can get a variation financial hardship arrangement where you and your lender agree to alter the terms of your loan agreement.
  • Talk to a financial counsellor. You can call the National Debt Helpline at 1800 007 007, this service is confidential and at no cost to you.

Planning For Retirement

It's finally time to sit down and relax. Preparing for retirement should be a smooth process. Here are a few things to consider that can make this transition easy as possible can be.
  • Figure out when exactly you plan to retire.
  • Figure out where you will be living, will you be renting? Have a house you own? Or be paying a mortgage?
  • Figure out what kind of lifestyle you want to live. Your expenses will greatly differ based on how you want to live moving forward.
  • Figure out your income. Will it be in the form of government benefits, continued employment or from your super? and how much will you be earning?

These are all the minimum pieces of information needed to make the retirement process run smoothly, leaving you stress free.

Downsizing
Planning For Retirement Downsizing

Downsizing

Heading into retirement downsizing your property must be weighing on your mind. If you decide to go down this route it will bring its own sets of benefits but also a few shortfalls. Here are a few factors to consider whether this would be right for you.
Pros

Firstly consider your lifestyle. Moving forward into retirement you may choose to start living a bit differently to how you have been up until this point. It might be worth downsizing so that day to day living is a bit more convenient by having a smaller place to maintain and look after. In addition to this an increase in money at hand after selling is not something to scoff at, and can potentially make life a lot easier afterwards.

Cons

The downsides is that moving comes with it's own set of obvious challenges. Losing a place you have lived in for so long and the emotional connection you have with it, along with the community and people you have been living with. You would be potentially starting anew, which has its positives and negatives.
